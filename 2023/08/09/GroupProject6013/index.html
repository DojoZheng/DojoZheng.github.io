GroupProject6013

2023/8/9 Study MBA Accounting

1. Group Project

For the group project, you are required to evaluate a company of your choice. For the evaluation, there should be use of financial statement analysis （财务分析） and valuation techniques. You are required to work in groups of 5 students.

Each group will be required to submit a project report (of at most 30 pages, normal page margin, Times New Roman font, font size 12, double-line spacing and including figures) and make a PowerPoint presentation on the final day of class (i.e., Class Day 4). Each student in the group is expected to present for about 3 minutes.

2. 英伟达 or 伯克希尔？

2.1 GPT的答案

从会计的角度来看，英伟达（NVIDIA）和伯克希尔哈撒韦（Berkshire Hathaway）都是具有复杂业务结构和广泛投资组合的公司，因此在做出一份30页的报告时，两家公司都可能需要深入的分析。然而，以下是一些可能影响你选择的因素：

1. 公司结构和业务领域：

  • 伯克希尔哈撒韦的业务范围广泛，涵盖金融、保险、能源、制造等领域，可能需要更多的分析来理解其不同业务部门的会计特点和风险。
  • 英伟达是一家以半导体和人工智能技术为核心的公司，其业务可能相对较窄，但在技术领域的复杂性可能需要深入分析。

2. 会计政策和复杂性：

  • 伯克希尔哈撒韦由于其多元化的业务和投资组合，可能涉及多种会计政策和估值方法，需要仔细研究不同领域的会计准则和处理方法。
  • 英伟达作为技术公司，其研发费用、资产计量等会计政策也可能涉及复杂性，需要深入理解。

3. 财务报表的详细性：

  • 伯克希尔哈撒韦的年度报告和股东信函通常提供广泛的信息，可能需要更多的时间来分析各个业务部门的细节和风险。
  • 英伟达也有详细的财务报告，但其核心业务可能相对明确，可能会在某些方面更容易进行深入分析。

4. 信息的可获得性：

  • 伯克希尔哈撒韦作为一家上市公司，其财务信息更易于获得，但由于其多元化的业务，信息可能分散在多个领域，可能需要更多的研究来整合数据。
  • 英伟达也是上市公司，但作为科技公司，其业务可能更容易理解，但需要深入分析技术领域。

总之，无论你选择哪家公司进行报告，都需要充分的研究和分析。伯克希尔哈撒韦的多元化业务和广泛的投资组合可能会导致更多的深入分析，而英伟达的技术核心业务可能相对明确，但仍需要对技术和会计政策有更深入的理解。最终的选择取决于你对这两家公司的兴趣、领域知识以及分析的深度。

2.2 个人总结

如果是研究伯克希尔，会是一个比较 广泛、但是 浅显 的分析，需要串联起来各个业务，总结出对公司的发展和影响；
如果是研究英伟达，会是一个比较狭窄、但是 深入 的分析，需要对电子芯片有非常深入的了解和洞见，总结出该行业未来发展的趋势和公司的前景。

如何选择？

  • 从分工的角度来讲，伯克希尔可能比较好分配工作，业务好拆分；
  • 从初学者的角度出发，个人认为伯克希尔会更合适一些，能够从会计的角度，以投资公司作为切入点，去看各个行业的特点；
  • 从投资者的角度出发，伯克希尔也会更有优势，能够帮助我们建立价值投资体系；
  • 从财报分析的角度来讲，英伟达可能比较好分析，因为不需要涉及太多、太复杂的业务；
  • 从未来人工智能对人们和产业的影响来讲，英伟达显然更合适；
  • 从课程项目能否拿高分来讲，值得大家探讨！

3. Coverage of the Report

3.1 基本要求

英文：

  1. Provide understanding of the business model of the company
  2. Consider the risk factors facing the company.
  3. Conduct a critical analysis (e.g., SWOT analysis) of the company.
  4. Evaluate the accounting and financial performance of the company.
    (to be discussed in Class Day 2)
  5. Assess the intrinsic value of equity through the process of business valuation with sensitivity analysis.
    (to be discussed in Class Day 2)

中文：

  1. 提供对公司商业模式的理解
  2. 考虑公司面临的风险因素（如PEST分析、波特五力模型Porter Five Forces Model）
  3. 对公司进行批判性分析（如SWOT分析）
  4. 评估公司的会计和财务业绩
  5. 用敏感性分析来评估股权的内在价值

3.2 进阶要求

Can you analyze XXXCompany from the following perspectives and give me a concise but complete content of a 30-pages report?

  1. Provide understanding of the business model of the company. It should include the Nature of business, Microeconomic environment and Macroeconomic environment.
  2. Consider the risk factors facing the company （e.g., PEST analysis and Porter Five Forces Model). It should include the Operational risk, economic risk, financial risk, political and regulatory risk.
  3. Conduct a critical analysis (e.g., SWOT analysis) of the company.
  4. Evaluate the accounting and financial performance of the company. It should include a horizontal analysis of financial statements, a vertical analysis of financial statements. What’s more, you should compute and evaluate the standard financial ratios.
  5. Assess the intrinsic value of equity through the process of business valuation with sensitivity analysis. It should include two main techniques: comparable company analysis and discounted cash flow analysis
  6. Analyze the company from the perspective of advising an equity investor and give recommendation

A. Berkshire Hathaway

Creating a concise yet complete 30-page report on Berkshire Hathaway requires summarizing the key points for each section. Here’s an overview of what each section could entail:

1. Business Model and Economic Environment (Approx. 3 pages):

  • Describe Berkshire Hathaway’s diversified business model, highlighting its insurance, investment, and operating subsidiaries.
  • Analyze the microeconomic environment by discussing its exposure to various industries and sectors.
  • Discuss the macroeconomic factors that influence Berkshire Hathaway’s business, such as interest rates, inflation, and overall economic trends.

2. Risk Factors Analysis (Approx. 4 pages):

  • Perform a PEST analysis to assess political, economic, social, and technological factors affecting the company.
  • Apply Porter’s Five Forces Model to evaluate the competitive landscape and industry dynamics.
  • Analyze operational, economic, financial, political, and regulatory risks specific to Berkshire Hathaway.

3. SWOT Analysis (Approx. 3 pages):

  • Evaluate the company’s strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
  • Highlight its strong brand, investment portfolio, and management expertise as strengths.
  • Identify areas like potential industry disruptions or regulatory changes as threats.

4. Financial Performance Analysis (Approx. 8 pages):

  • Conduct a horizontal analysis of the company’s income statement and balance sheet over the past few years to identify trends.
  • Perform a vertical analysis to assess the composition of assets, liabilities, and equity.
  • Compute and evaluate key financial ratios such as liquidity, solvency, profitability, and efficiency ratios.

5. Business Valuation (Approx. 7 pages):

  • Explain the importance of business valuation and the methods used.
  • Conduct a Comparable Company Analysis, selecting relevant peers and comparing valuation multiples.
  • Perform a Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) analysis, projecting future cash flows and discounting them to arrive at an intrinsic value estimate.
  • Conduct sensitivity analysis to gauge how changes in key assumptions affect the valuation.

6. Equity Investor Recommendation (Approx. 5 pages):

  • Based on the analysis, provide an equity investor perspective on Berkshire Hathaway’s attractiveness as an investment.
  • Weigh the strengths and risks identified in previous sections to make an informed recommendation.
  • Consider factors like investment horizon, risk tolerance, and potential returns.
  • Provide a balanced recommendation, potentially discussing long-term growth prospects, potential challenges, and the overall risk-return profile.

Remember, this is a condensed overview of the content for each section. For a comprehensive report, you would need to expand on each point, provide data, charts, and additional insights, and ensure that your analysis is well-supported and coherent.
